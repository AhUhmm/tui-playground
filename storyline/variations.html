Color Logic:

Operational (RGB Primaries)

RED || Tool
GREEN -- Read
BLUE == Write

Cognitive (CMY Secondaries)

CYAN /\ Think
YELLOW \/ Decision
MAGENTA <> Pivot

System (High Contrast)

WHITE XX Error - Maximum visibility, stands out among all colors

Rationale: RGB primaries represent concrete operations (actions on the world), CMY secondaries represent abstract thinking (mental processes). White error creates maximum contrast.

Color Theory Reference:

RGB (Additive primaries):        CMY (Subtractive primaries):
  Red   = #FF0000                  Cyan    = #00FFFF (R+G complement)
  Green = #00FF00                  Magenta = #FF00FF (R+B complement)
  Blue  = #0000FF                  Yellow  = #FFFF00 (G+B complement)

Used for: Operational actions    Used for: Cognitive processes
(concrete, external)             (abstract, internal)

System alerts: White #FFFFFF (maximum contrast on dark background)

Science-based: Warm colors (low Kelvin) advance visually and trigger arousal/action. Cool colors (high Kelvin) recede and promote calm/receptive states. High-saturation colors (yellow, purple) demand attention for critical decision points. White-on-red creates maximum urgency response.

Perception Principles Applied:

1. Visual Advancing/Receding (Chromatic Aberration)
   Warm colors (red, orange) appear closer ‚Üí Active operations
   Cool colors (blue, green) appear farther ‚Üí Passive/receptive

2. Arousal Response (Psychological Impact)
   Warm: ‚Üë arousal, urgency, focus on detail (Tool, Write)
   Cool: ‚Üì arousal, calm, creative thinking (Think, Read)

3. Attention Capture (Saturation + Hue)
   Yellow: Maximum visibility, decision-making trigger
   Purple: Rare in nature, signals unusual event (pivot)
   White on Red: Danger pattern, immediate action required

4. Color Temperature (Kelvin Scale)
   2500K (warm) ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí 7000K (cool)
   Action-oriented        Contemplative

5. Temporal Attention Pattern
   Warm colors: Attention at START of process (initiate)
   Cool colors: Attention at END of process (receive/analyze)
